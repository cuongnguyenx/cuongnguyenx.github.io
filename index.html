Hello!

I'm a masters student at Georgia Tech studying computer science, with a specialization in Social Computing. Previously, I graduated with bachelors degree (highest honors) in Computer Science and Mathematics, also from Georgia Tech. I'm very lucky to be advised by Prof. Munmun De Choudhury in the SOCWEB Lab and Prof. Michael Best in the TID Lab

My research interests are at the intersection of human computer interaction, social computing and machine learning. I follow a theory-guided approach, combined with techniques from the aforementioned subfields, to extract ecologically valid insights and design interventions utilizing such insights for domains with significant impact on personal and societal well-being. These have included (but will not be limited to) human-AI complementarity, online content moderation, misinformation and hate speech, digital mental health and clinical decision support systems.

Previously, I have worked at Intuit as a software engineering intern on the Reusable AI Experiences team, and a research asssistant for Purdue's AKRaNLU Lab on computational humor
